# Predicting Measles Outbreaks
Team project: summer26-measles-outbreaks-predictions

This is a project for the Summer 2026 Erdős Institute Data Science program.

# Overview
Measles has long been regarded as a 'canary in the coal mine' due to its exceptionally contagious nature: measles is typically the first disease to spike when vaccination rates decline. As measles begins to spread throughout the USA, we use data provided by the Centers for Disease Control and local governments to predict when and where the next measles spike will happen.

# Stakeholders
The primary stakeholders for this project include public health agencies, policymakers, and local communities in the United States of America who may use this analysis to help prioritize when and where to create vaccination campaigns, increase hospital capacity, etc. More broadly, this modeling framework may also be useful to researchers and public health organizations in other countries or regions who are also at risk of losing their measles eradication status.
